The curators of an exhibition at the Schirn Kunsthalle in Frankfurt describe the Montmartre of that time, situated on the outscirts of Paris, as a rural counter-world to the metropolis in the centre of Paris. The exhibition is presenting paintings and photographies, showing a life between Moulin Rouge and coffeehouses, between the poor and the outsiders.
