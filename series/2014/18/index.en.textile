Self-portraits with mirrors, arranged portraits and pictorial compositions as shots from unusual perspectives are typical motives of the American-Swiss photographer Florence Henri (1893-1982). She started out as a painter in the Parisienne art scene and found access to photography during a guest semester at the Bauhaus in Dessau. Through her openness to new trends, she developed her own experimental style in the 20s of the last century. 
The exhibition at the Pinakothek shows about 65 photographs by Henri as well as historical documents and publications from the Ann and Jürgen Wilde Foundation.
