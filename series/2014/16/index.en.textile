He was a multifaceted artist, court sculptor and castle builder and he brought as the "Michelangelo of the North" in the early 17th century baroque splendor to Berlin. Almost threatened Andreas Schlüter still to be forgotten, partly because of its architectural masterpiece, the "Berliner Schloss" was demolished in 1950. But the palace is being rebuilt...
...and the Bode Museum is dedicated to the major artists of the occasion of his 300th death year, a comprehensive exhibition, with a handwritten drawing by Schlüter, a Prometheus-miniature.
