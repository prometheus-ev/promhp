Since Saturday, Germany has the 39th World Heritage Site. After a few years of application, the former abbey of Corvey in the Westphalian Hoexter is now recognized by the Commission of Unesco as a cultural and natural heritage. 
The Benedictine abbey, dating from the 9th century, belonged in the Middle Ages with its school and library to the spiritual and religious center of the Frankish Empire. The Carolingian West plant, built 873-885, is a brick building, oriented to the west, with two facade towers and one central tower. 

Congratulations!
