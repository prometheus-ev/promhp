The photojournalist Margaret Bourke-White (1904-1971) travelled all over the world in search of historic moments. She documented the droughts of 1934 in the USA, the German invasion of the Soviet Union 1941 and was in Germany when the war ended. She took photos of the allied bombardments, of the destroyed cities and the liberation of the Buchenwald concentration camp. 
As a women in a male-dominated business Margaret Bourke-White became subject of the press. Particularly popular was this portrait, showing her in flying gear.
