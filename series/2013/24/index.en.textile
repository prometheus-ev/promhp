Uruk, now Warka situated in south of Iraq, is the first known metropolis. Since November 1912 there are German excavations in Uruk. At that time it was customary to divide the archaeological finds, so that an amount of objects made their way to Germany, to the Museum of the Ancient Near East at Berlin and the Uruk-Warka's Collection of the German Archaeological Institute at Heidelberg. Scores of finds from both collections are now presented in an exhibition concerning genesis and flourishing of Uruk.
