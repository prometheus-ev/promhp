Last Sunday ended an exhibition in the Hamburger Kunsthalle with works of Ronald B. Kitaj. The paintings of Kitaj are colourful, include quotations and belong – with elements of cubism, surrealism and dadaism – to a very versatile figural painting. Kitaj, an artist of Jewish descent, who lived in Amerika and Europe, developed the idea of an “artistic diasporism”. The cafés in the cities, where displaced persons and travellers come and go, were throughout his life an important source of inspiration.
