When the Picture Gallery in Sanssouci Park was finished in 1763, it was one of the first buildings in Europe exclusively erected for an art collection and one of the most magnificent in its kind as well. 250 years after the opening an exhibition in the elaborately restored picture gallery evokes the original interior concept of King Frederick II. The effort to show the historical hanging can be seen in the small cabinet; ancient sculptures, which have not been in the gallery since 1830, could be won as loans from Berlin and Poznan and complete the show.
