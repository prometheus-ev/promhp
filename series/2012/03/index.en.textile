The motifs of pictures made by Hans Baluschek (1870 – 1935) are reflections of the time around 1900. They show the working life and the freetime of people living in a metropolis, they show industrial landscapes and urban landscapes, but illustrations of fairy tales, too, like „Peter and Anneli's Journey to the Moon“ by Gerd von Bassewitz.  
The Börhan-Museum in Berlin has an extensive collection of artworks by this member of the secession in Berlin and is currently presenting an exhibition with 40 pieces of his early work.
