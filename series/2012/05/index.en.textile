Ferdinand Hodler (1853 – 1918) and Cuno Amiet (1868 – 1961) shaped the advent of the art nouveau movement in Switzerland at the turn of the century. Their friendship, which is described as one between art nouveau and modern art, is subject of an exhibition in Hamburg. The two artists knew each other since 1893 and were in a lively artistic exchange, which is directly comprehensible at some groups of work.
