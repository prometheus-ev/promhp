The name “Naumburg Master“ refers to a sculptor and his workshop with an outstanding reputation throughout medieval Europe.
A comprehensive exhibition in Naumburg deals with the formation and education of the workshop in the 1220s at cathedrals situated in Île de France, Champagne and Picardie as well as with the dissemination of their work via Mainz to Naumburg and Meissen. Another section of the exhibition is dedicated to the reception in the 19th and 20th century, when the work of the French, for example with the sculpture of Uta, was declared as typical German.
