By mid-may an exhibition will be over, which presents unknown watercolours of the late work of Karl Schmidt-Rottluff. 
It seems that watercolour was predestined for the spontaneous, colourful and simplifyingway of painting by Schmidt-Rottluff. There are watercolours from the early years, when the artist group Brücke was founded, to the late 1960th. In the exhibition the watercolours were complemented with a selection of pen-and-ink drawings and prints, like the woodcut “Melancholie”.
