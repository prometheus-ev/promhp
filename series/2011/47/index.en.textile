An exhibition in Kassel deals with the presentation of light in art. The main focus is on the 17th century, when light became a central aspect in painting. The painting of the 15th and 16th century is shown as the origin: The innovation of Caravaggio was adopted and developed in the north of the Alps for example by Gerard van Honthorst. Paintings of Rubens, Rembrandt and Vermeer as well as contemporary art are analysed – with a side glance on contemporary natural scientific developments too.
