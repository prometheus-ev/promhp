The human skull was attendant on different meanings in the history.
It serves as a relic in connection with hagiolatry in the Middle Ages, as a symbol of transience in the Baroque and today as a method of provocation and fashionable accessory.

The artist and darwinist Gabriel von Max (1840 â€“ 1915) had with around 500 skull-objects one of the biggest collections, among the labelled skull of Andreas Fries from Prien at the Chiemsee, who died in 1823. In 1917 the collection was bought by the corporation Mannheim, but was regarded as lost after the second world war. As recently as 2008 it was found again and now it is constituting the exhibition in Mannheim basically.
