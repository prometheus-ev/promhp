In the years after 1910 the Liebermann family used to spend the summer at Wannsee Berlin. During that time over 200 canvas were being painted, all of them showing themes of Liebermann's garden surrounding his summer residence. Liebermann had designed the garden with its hedge garden, avenue of birches, flower garden, tea pavillon and kitchen garden together with his friend Alfred Lichtwark, the director of the Kunsthalle Hamburg.

In addition to the exhibition a reflection of this garden is being presented on the roof of the Bundeskunsthalle. The garden's three main elements – hedge garden, birch avenue and flower garden - as well as the garden's visual axes are freely arranged.
