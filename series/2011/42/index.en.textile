The first retrospektive to the corpus of Willem de Kooning took place in 1965 at the Smith College Museum of Art in Northhampton. The current retrospective at the Museum of Modern Art with around 200 paintings, sculptures, drawings, and prints exceeds that one and comprises the early academic works, made before he moved to the United States in 1926 to the final, abstract paintings of the late 80s. 
De Kooning is regarded as one of the most important exponents of the abtract expressionism. With Jackson Pollock and Mark Rothko he was a part of that artist generation, who turned New York into a capitel of art after the second world war.
